Marin Software alternatives for unified paid media (2026)

Marin's MarinOne unifies paid search, social, retail media, and app advertising — but a 2025 restructuring has teams re-evaluating their options. Here are the active alternatives, and how to judge each one.

Marin Software markets MarinOne as a unified platform for managing performance media — bringing paid search, paid social, retail media, and app advertising into a single interface to manage and automate ad platforms at scale. It connects to major publishers including Google, Meta, Amazon, and Apple Search Ads, and lets advertisers export data to a BI platform of choice. Its product line spans Marin Connect for data aggregation, Marin Ascend for AI-driven optimization and forecasting, MarinOne for campaign automation at scale, and Marin for Agencies.

One thing to factor into any evaluation: in 2025, Marin's legacy public company filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in the District of Delaware (filed July 1, 2025) and emerged from reorganization on September 5, 2025, with all prior common stock cancelled and new equity issued to new owners. The company's own site now positions Marin under new ownership entering an "AI-first era," and continues to market MarinOne, Marin Connect, and Marin Ascend as available products. The brand is best treated as active-but-restructured rather than a stable incumbent — which is reason enough for many teams to look at what else is out there.

The main alternatives

Each of these is a genuinely active platform. They differ in where they focus — commerce media, creative-plus-buying, autonomous optimization, or rule-based PPC management — so the right fit depends on the channels you run and how much control you want to keep.

  • Skai (formerly Kenshoo) — an omnichannel platform for commerce media that centralizes media data and lets brands and agencies plan, activate, and measure paid search, paid social, and retail media across walled-garden publishers from one system. A natural look for teams that liked MarinOne's unified, publisher-spanning scope, especially where retail media matters. (Worth knowing: Skai is the current name for the platform formerly known as Kenshoo.)
  • Smartly (smartly.io) — an AI advertising platform that unifies creative production and media buying across social, Connected TV, video, and the open web. Fits teams whose bottleneck is creative throughput alongside media buying, rather than search-led management.
  • Trapica — an AI-powered marketing automation platform that autonomously optimizes campaign targeting, bidding, and budget allocation across Meta, Google, TikTok, and other ad channels. Fits teams that want hands-off, autonomous optimization across social and search channels.
  • Optmyzr — a PPC management platform with rule-based automation, monitoring, auditing, and reporting for Google Ads, Microsoft Ads, Amazon Ads, Meta Ads, and LinkedIn Ads. Fits practitioners who want explicit, rule-based control and reporting over search and shopping accounts rather than a fully autonomous system.

Where campaignautomation.ai fits

campaignautomation.ai sits in the same category as a deliberately different option: the bounded-autonomy peer. Instead of moving your spend into a vendor's platform, it builds and governs autonomous optimization on your own ad and marketing accounts — so you own the system that results.

The approach is built around three principles. Every automated change is logged as Trigger / Action / Impact — the condition that fired, the change that was made, and the measured result — so nothing is a black box. Actions run inside guardrails you set: spend caps, change ceilings, brand and keyword exclusions, and approval thresholds. Changes are reversible, and the connection starts read-only and auditable. The phrase we use is "autonomy you can audit." It's delivered through a free Readiness Score, a paid Campaign Automation Audit as the first sprint, and Automation Sprints that build one workflow at a time.

How to choose

Most of these tools will demo well. The useful lens is not "how smart is the AI" but how much of the execution you can see and steer — what we call the execution-depth spectrum. Whichever tool you shortlist, ask the same three questions:

  • Can it explain? When the platform changes a bid, budget, or audience, can it show you the condition that triggered the change and the result it produced — or do you just see a moved number?
  • Can it bound? Can you set hard limits — spend caps, change ceilings, exclusions, approval gates — that the automation cannot cross, before it touches a live account?
  • Can it reverse? If an automated change goes the wrong way, can you roll it back cleanly, and is there an audit trail of what happened?

Match those answers to the channels you actually run. Skai and Smartly lean toward unified, large-scale commerce and creative-plus-media operations; Trapica leans autonomous across social and search; Optmyzr leans explicit and rule-based. And factor Marin's restructuring into your risk assessment rather than ignoring it.

Last reviewed June 2026. These platforms change frequently — confirm current capabilities and pricing on each vendor's own site.
