Clay AI features: Claygent, Sculptor, and waterfall enrichment explained

Clay positions its AI around four named capabilities — Claygent, Sculptor, Navigator, and waterfall enrichment — that research accounts and fill in contact data. Here's what each one does, and how to evaluate them before you turn an agent loose on your pipeline.

What Clay's AI actually is

Clay (clay.com) builds its AI feature set around go-to-market work: researching target companies and people, then filling in the data your team needs to act. The flagship is Claygent, Clay's AI research agent. Clay positions Claygent to research target companies and people with AI as part of GTM workflows — searching the web, orchestrating workflows, and creating content, all connected to your first- and third-party data.

In practice, that means Claygent can take an account or contact you already have and go find the unstructured details that don't live in a tidy database — Clay describes it as a built-in web research agent that surfaces information from public sources such as job listings, press releases, and company pages, driven by natural-language prompts.

Navigator, Claygent Builder, and model choice

Most research tools only read pages. Navigator is a Claygent capability that lets the agent interact with web pages — applying filters, filling out search forms, clicking buttons, and retrieving structured data from sites that don't expose their data easily. That moves Claygent from passive reading toward acting on a page the way a person would.

You build and manage these agents in Claygent Builder, which Clay describes as the centralized hub for building, testing, and deploying Claygents — AI agents designed for judgment-based GTM work such as account research, lead scoring, outbound copywriting, and persona classification. A useful detail for buyers: inside Claygent Builder you can swap between different AI models (Clay names Claude and GPT-4o as examples) in the configuration panel to test output quality without changing your prompt.

Sculptor and waterfall enrichment

Sculptor is Clay's AI copilot. It takes your business context, guides workflow setup, recommends enrichments, and provides insights using natural language — and it can turn a plain-language description into a production-ready Claygent prompt. If Claygent is the agent that does the research, Sculptor is the layer that helps you configure it without writing the prompt from scratch.

Separately, waterfall enrichment is core to Clay's value even though it isn't strictly an "AI" feature. It queries data providers sequentially until a valid match is found, and stops once a provider returns a valid data point. It supports work emails, personal emails, and mobile phone numbers, and can be applied to additional data points such as funding stage, tech stack, job openings, and recent news. Clay connects this to a marketplace of 150+ data providers and databases accessible in one place.

Reading Clay through a bounded-autonomy lens

An agent that searches the web, fills forms, clicks buttons, and writes copy is doing real work on your behalf — which is exactly where you want guardrails. Our framing is Trigger, Action, Impact: for any automation, you should be able to name what set it off, what it did, and what changed as a result. Apply that to Claygent. The trigger is your prompt and the rows it runs against. The action is the research or page interaction Navigator performs. The impact is the data written back into your tables and, downstream, the outbound it informs.

The buyer's job is to keep all three visible. Claygent Builder's test-and-deploy flow and model-swap panel help here — you can validate output before deploying — and that's the kind of guardrail-driven setup we'd push for. Treat agentic research the way you'd treat any system acting in your stack: review what it produces before it feeds a sequence, and keep a human checkpoint where judgment matters. This is our lens for evaluating any vendor, not a claim about Clay specifically.

Last reviewed June 2026. Clay updates its AI capabilities frequently — confirm current specifics on the vendor's own site.
