The AI features of the top marketing automation platforms, compared (2026)

Five major platforms now ship AI under their own brand names — but they don't mean the same thing by it. Some surface suggestions for a human to approve; others let agents send email, route journeys, and manage ad spend on their own. Here's a grounded read on each, organized around how much each one lets AI act versus assist.

Every marketing automation vendor now has an "AI" page. The hard part for a buyer isn't finding AI — it's working out what the AI is actually allowed to do. There's a real difference between a feature that drafts a subject line for you to approve and an agent that continues an email thread with a prospect, reroutes a customer's journey, or adjusts ad spend in real time. Both get called "AI." Only one of them takes an irreversible action on your behalf.

So the useful way to compare these platforms isn't a feature checklist — it's a spectrum from assist (the AI proposes, a human disposes) to act (the AI executes inside the live system). Below is a fair, source-grounded summary of where each of five major platforms sits today. We're not naming an objective winner; the right answer depends on what you're trying to do and how much autonomy you're prepared to grant. At the end, we offer one lens — bounded autonomy — for deciding among any of them.

HubSpot (Breeze)

HubSpot has unified its AI under the brand Breeze, organized into three pillars: Breeze Assistant (a conversational interface that answers questions and completes tasks grounded in your CRM data and role), Breeze Agents (automated agents), and Breeze Intelligence (data enrichment and buyer-intent scoring drawn from a database of company and contact profiles to fill missing CRM fields).

The agents are where Breeze leans toward acting: the generally available lineup includes a Customer Agent that resolves inquiries and qualifies leads across channels, a Prospecting Agent that monitors accounts for buying signals and builds personalized outreach for a rep to review and send, and a Data Agent that answers questions about customers using CRM data and documents. Worth noting on maturity: several additional agents — including Company Research, Customer Health, and a Closing Agent — are listed in beta, so capability and general availability vary by feature. Full breakdown of HubSpot's Breeze AI features.

Salesforce (Agentforce Marketing / Einstein)

Salesforce sits furthest toward the act end. At Dreamforce 2025 it announced that the next generation of Marketing Cloud is now Agentforce Marketing — the Marketing Cloud line rebranded under the Agentforce brand. (If you're evaluating "Marketing Cloud," that's the current name to search for.)

The agentic features are explicit about taking action: a Two-Way Email capability lets Agentforce continue email threads or proactively start conversations, replacing one-way "Do Not Reply" sends; Journey Decisioning automatically routes each customer to their next-best journey based on real-time behavior; Adaptive Web experiences tailor web content from a live agentic conversation without manual configuration; and Paid Media Optimization monitors and manages ad performance across platforms, taking real-time actions. Underneath, Einstein persists as the foundational predictive and trust layer — a prerequisite for the agents rather than something the rebrand retired. Full breakdown of Salesforce's Agentforce and Einstein AI.

Adobe Marketo Engage

Marketo Engage is pushing hard into agentic territory, with an architecture that's notably open. The Marketo MCP Server acts as a secure hub connecting AI agents and workflows directly to Marketo data and processes, and supports connecting external AI tools via the Model Context Protocol — Adobe's documentation names clients such as Claude and OpenAI Codex, and notes any MCP-compatible client can connect. Callable Agents run inside Smart Campaign flow steps to perform tasks such as program QA, lead and data normalization, lead investigation, campaign creation, and lead import — meaning the AI can act within workflows you design.

On the assist side, an Email Designer AI Assistant suggests subject lines, writes body copy, and selects images tailored to personas or buying stages, and a product-knowledge AI Assistant answers plain-language "how do I" questions with step-by-step instructions. Marketo is also introducing a conversational interface where a chat panel becomes the primary way to browse activities and give agents commands. AI-driven Sales Qualifier and Brand Concierge features are included in the Prime and Ultimate packages. Full breakdown of Marketo Engage's agentic AI.

ActiveCampaign (Active Intelligence)

ActiveCampaign unifies its AI under Active Intelligence, spanning machine learning and generative AI. It leans toward letting AI build while keeping a human in the loop: the AI Campaign Builder creates complete campaigns from a text description, and the AI Automation Builder constructs multi-step workflows — triggers, conditions, wait steps, actions — from a stated objective.

Predictive features include Predictive Sending (timing sends to when individual contacts are likeliest to open) and Win Probability (scoring how likely a deal is to close). A March 2026 addition, AI Performance Intelligence, analyzes campaign and automation performance to flag what's over- or under-performing and the creative, timing, and audience factors behind it. Custom AI instructions let you define brand voice and strategic preferences once so they apply across recommendations and builds — and agencies can manage those across client accounts. Other features include AI-Suggested Segments, an AI Brand Kit, a Reporting Co-Pilot, and Sentiment Analysis. Full breakdown of ActiveCampaign's Active Intelligence.

Oracle Eloqua (Advanced Intelligence)

Oracle Eloqua sits closest to the assist end. Its AI is the Advanced Intelligence Cloud Service — available to all customers since June 12, 2025, but opt-in: you enable it by opening a service request with Oracle Support. The feature set is largely generative and predictive rather than autonomous: Subject Line Generation produces up to five suggestions from a prompt (replacing the discontinued Subject Line Optimization), and Content Generation creates text or variations for emails and landing pages.

On the analytics side, Fatigue Analysis estimates how email-fatigued each contact is and stores it in a system field so you can adjust frequency; Account Intelligence assigns a 0–100 Account Engagement Score with a performance dashboard; and Send Time Optimization times sends to individual engagement patterns. Development is ongoing — the Eloqua 26B release (May 2026) added a conversational experience for subject line generation, including attaching reference documents, plus admin access controls for AI features. Full breakdown of Oracle Eloqua's Advanced Intelligence.

How to actually decide

Notice that the brand names don't tell you much. "Breeze," "Agentforce," "Active Intelligence," "Advanced Intelligence" — each spans everything from a subject-line suggester to an agent that emails your prospects. So the question that separates them isn't does it have AI, it's how much is the AI allowed to act, and on what.

Our lens for that is bounded autonomy: AI should act only inside guardrails you set, and every action it takes should be visible and reversible. A clean way to pressure-test any of these platforms is the Trigger → Action → Impact frame. For each AI feature you're considering, ask:

  • Trigger — what causes it to fire, and can you see and constrain that condition?
  • Action — what exactly does it do? A suggestion you approve, or a live send / journey change / ad-spend adjustment that happens without you?
  • Impact — is the action logged, explainable, and reversible if it's wrong?

Run that against the spectrum above and the trade-off gets concrete. Eloqua's generative assists and ActiveCampaign's builders mostly produce drafts a human ships, so the blast radius of a mistake is small. Salesforce's two-way email and journey decisioning, HubSpot's outbound Prospecting Agent, and Marketo's Callable Agents can act on live contacts — higher leverage, and a stronger reason to insist on guardrails and read-only, auditable access first. Neither end is "better." The right pick is the one whose autonomy matches what you're comfortable letting run unattended — and where you can explain, bound, and reverse what it does.

Last reviewed June 2026. These platforms change their AI capabilities frequently — confirm current specifics on each vendor's own site.
