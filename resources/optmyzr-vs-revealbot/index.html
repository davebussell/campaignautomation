Optmyzr vs. Revealbot

Two well-known names in PPC automation, built for different channels and different jobs. One key thing to know up front: Revealbot is now Bïrch. Here is how the two approaches compare and who each one fits.

Search "Optmyzr vs Revealbot" and you will find two automation platforms that look similar on the surface and diverge quickly underneath. They were built for different advertising channels, organized around different workflows, and aimed at different kinds of buyers. Before comparing them, one detail matters: Revealbot has been rebranded. The company announced on October 21, 2024 that it is now Bïrch — the same team, platform, and core capabilities, described as reflecting a shift in product philosophy rather than only a name change. The platform now operates exclusively as Bïrch and no longer uses the Revealbot name, though many third-party sources still write it as "Birch (Revealbot)." This page compares the two fairly and points to a third, governed approach for buyers who care most about auditable control.

Optmyzr

Optmyzr is a PPC management and optimization software platform for marketers. It was founded August 9, 2013 and is headquartered in Mountain View, California, by Frederick Vallaeys, Geetanjali Tyagi, and Manas Garg. The platform supports multiple ad networks — Google Ads, Microsoft Advertising, Amazon Ads, Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads, and Yahoo! Japan Ads — organized into Optmyzr for Search, for Amazon, and for Social.

Its core capabilities center on a customizable Rule Engine for automation, real-time campaign monitoring with alerts, bid and budget management, account audits, one-click optimizations, and automated reporting. Optmyzr positions itself for the AI and automation era of PPC, offering an AI Sidekick copilot and LLM-supported optimization suggestions, with users choosing when to apply AI features. Notably, it describes itself as a tool for marketers rather than a marketing service provider, and it is an official partner with Google, Microsoft, Meta, and Amazon.

Who it fits: teams and agencies running search-heavy programs across Google, Microsoft, and Amazon who want deep optimization controls, audits, and reporting, while deciding for themselves when AI suggestions get applied.

Revealbot (now Bïrch)

Revealbot was originally founded in New York City in 2016. It began as a Facebook Ads automation layer, providing hourly data refreshes and customizable rules for DTC brands. As of the October 2024 rebrand, it operates as Bïrch, a performance marketing automation platform and an official partner of Meta, Google Ads, Snapchat Ads, and TikTok Ads.

Its core functions include rule-based campaign management with Smart Rules, ad automation and scaled launches, bulk creative testing, tracking, and analytics with AI-powered Highlights. The lineage shows in the channel mix: its strength sits with social and short-form video platforms, and the move to Bïrch reflects a shift toward a creative-plus-performance philosophy rather than a change in the underlying team or platform.

Who it fits: social-first and DTC advertisers running Meta, TikTok, Snapchat, and Google who want rule-driven scaling and bulk creative testing in one place — under the current Bïrch brand.

Where each wins

The honest read is that these tools win on different terrain rather than head to head. Optmyzr's edge is breadth and depth on search and marketplace channels — Google, Microsoft, and Amazon — with a Rule Engine, account audits, and reporting built for marketers who want granular optimization and the choice of when AI steps in. If your spend concentrates in search and shopping, that is where it concentrates too.

Bïrch (formerly Revealbot) wins on social and short-form video. Its roots in Facebook Ads automation and its current partnerships with Meta, TikTok, Snapchat, and Google make it a natural fit for advertisers whose growth depends on rule-based scaling and high-volume creative testing across those feeds. The two overlap on Google and on the shared principle of rule-based automation, but their centers of gravity are clearly different. The right pick follows your channel mix and which workflow — search optimization or social creative scaling — defines your day.

A third option: governed automation

Both platforms run on rules and suggestions you configure. A related but distinct approach is governed automation — bounded autonomy, where AI acts on your own accounts inside guardrails you set. The defining principles: every change is logged as Trigger, Action, and Impact; actions are reversible; and the system is read-only and auditable first. The idea is autonomy you can audit, for buyers who want automation to move fast without losing a clear record of what changed and why. campaignautomation.ai is one option in this space to consider alongside the two tools above.

If that framing appeals to you, the Trigger / Action / Impact model shows what an auditable change log looks like in practice, and the execution depth spectrum maps where rule-based tools, copilots, and bounded-autonomy systems sit relative to one another.
