Cognism AI features: sales intelligence and intent data

Cognism's AI runs on a proprietary engine called Cortex AI, surfaced through its Sales Companion prospecting suite. Here's what the features do, where the data comes from, and the questions to ask before you let any of it act for you.

What Cognism's AI actually is

Cognism's current AI engine is branded Cortex AI, a proprietary system the company describes as built on a "Prompt Engine" and "governed by design" to prioritize trust, accuracy, and relevance across its outputs. Cortex is the engine; you don't interact with it directly so much as through the features it powers.

Those features live inside Sales Companion, a prospecting suite Cognism launched on March 18, 2025. Sales Companion unifies verified contact data, AI research, buying signals, and CRM workflows in one place, and provides access to Cognism's database of 440M+ contacts and 100M+ mobile numbers. The AI sits on top of that data rather than alongside it.

The three named Cortex features

AI Search lets sellers build contact and account lists using natural-language prompts instead of manual filters. You describe who you want in plain language — and it supports a voice input mode as well as typed queries — and the tool assembles the list for you.

Smart Personas takes a single product or use-case description and generates 3-5 structured buyer-persona profiles, automatically suggesting titles, seniority, department, and region. It's a starting point for targeting rather than a finished segmentation.

Research by Cortex is a one-click company research tool inside Sales Companion. It compiles pre-call briefs covering the company description and revenue model, strategic priorities, competitive landscape, and ice-breakers — with embedded source links drawn from public data. The "Cortex" branding is Cognism's own engine name, documented in the company's product updates, so when you see "Research by Cortex AI," that's the engine name, not a third-party label.

Intent and signal data under the hood

Cognism's intent data is powered by a partnership with Bombora, providing topic-level intent signals — both general and location-based — that indicate interest in a solution similar to yours. This is third-party intent: useful for prioritization, but worth understanding as a directional signal rather than a confirmed in-market buyer.

Beyond intent, Cognism's Signal Data surfaces a wider set of buying signals:

  • Job changes and hiring trends
  • Funding alerts, across 12 funding-alert types
  • Mergers and acquisitions
  • Technographics drawn from a database of over 20,000 technologies

These AI features and signals are accessible both in the web app and through a Sales Companion browser extension that works over LinkedIn and company websites, so the intelligence travels to where reps already prospect.

Reading Cognism through a bounded-autonomy lens

Most of what Cortex AI does is generative and read-oriented: it drafts personas, assembles lists, and writes pre-call briefs. That's a comfortable place to start, because the output lands in front of a human before anything happens. The questions sharpen once these outputs feed downstream actions — auto-built lists pushed into sequences, signals that trigger outreach.

We use a simple frame called Trigger, Action, Impact: what signal fired, what the system did in response, and what changed as a result. Cognism's cited-source briefs and embedded links are a genuine strength here — they make the trigger traceable. The discipline you bring is on the action side: decide which Cortex outputs a rep reviews versus which ones flow straight into your CRM, and keep that boundary explicit. That's the essence of guardrail-driven automation — let the AI reason and suggest inside limits you set, and keep every action visible and reversible.

What to confirm before you buy

If you're evaluating Cognism, separate the data layer from the AI layer. The data — verified contacts, mobile numbers, Bombora intent, and the signal types above — is the foundation. The Cortex features are accelerators on top of it. Pressure-test how each feature's output enters your workflow, where a human reviews it, and how you'd unwind an action that fired on a bad signal.

It's also worth checking exactly how intent topics map to your real buying committee, since topic-level signals are broad by nature, and how the persona and research outputs are sourced so your reps can defend what's in front of a prospect.

Last reviewed June 2026. Cognism updates its AI capabilities frequently — confirm current specifics on the vendor's own site.
