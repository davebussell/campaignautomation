Adobe Marketo Engage AI features

Adobe layers several distinct AI capabilities into Marketo Engage — older predictive models and a newer generative "Marketo AI" assistant. Here is what each one does, and what to keep under your control before you turn it on.

What "AI in Marketo Engage" actually refers to

When Adobe talks about AI in Marketo Engage — part of Adobe Experience Cloud — it is really talking about three separate things that arrived at different times. There is the long-standing Predictive Audiences and Predictive Content, and there is a newer layer Adobe brands as Marketo AI: an AI Assistant plus a set of agent skills. They are not one feature with one switch, so it helps to evaluate them separately.

The predictive features lean on machine learning to score and target people. The Marketo AI layer leans on generative AI to draft content and answer questions about your instance. Understanding which is which tells you where the model is making a prediction versus where it is producing something a human should review.

Predictive Audiences and Predictive Content

Predictive Audiences uses AI and machine learning to help you target the right people. Adobe automatically includes and activates it for instances on the Prime or Ultimate pricing bundles. It surfaces a handful of predictive filters: Likelihood to Attend and Likelihood to Register (for Event Programs only), Likelihood to Unsubscribe, and two Lookalike filters — Lookalike of Program Members and Lookalike of Smart List Members — available across program types.

The Lookalike filters expand an audience by finding leads similar to members of a program or Smart List, evaluating 50+ factors including lead attributes, email activity, web activity, and engagement. The Likelihood to Unsubscribe filter flags whether a lead has a high or low likelihood of unsubscribing in the next two weeks — useful for trimming who you mail before you fatigue a list.

Predictive Content sits alongside this. It serves machine-learning content recommendations through three sources — Email, Rich Media (web), and a Recommendation Bar — and its analytics track clicks on recommended content plus a conversion rate calculated as conversions divided by clicks.

The Marketo AI layer: assistant and agent skills

The newer Marketo AI capabilities are delivered as agent skills. These include Investigate leads, which gives a plain-language explanation of why a person did not reach a milestone; Product knowledge, which answers questions in plain language by drawing on official Adobe documentation, with responses grounded in and citing that documentation; Validate programs, which checks setup against best practices before launch; and Import leads, which handles lead-list import with field-mapping assistance.

Separately, the AI Assistant in the Email Designer uses generative AI to write subject lines and preheaders, create or modify individual email sections, and generate a full email from a template with a single prompt. It can generate images via Adobe Firefly, and it lets you personalize outputs by specifying buying group, journey stage, communication strategy, language, and tone — and upload brand assets as references.

Adobe also frames a broader agentic AI story for Marketo Engage: native agents, MCP connectivity, and callable agents triggered from Smart Campaigns, with an Adobe-hosted Marketo Engage MCP Server that bridges external AI assistants to your instance.

Where to set guardrails: the Trigger, Action, Impact view

This is our lens, not Adobe's: any AI you let into a marketing platform should be judged on three things — its Trigger (what set it off), its Action (what it did), and its Impact (what changed as a result). Most of Marketo's AI is comfortably bounded today. A predictive filter or a generated subject line is a suggestion a human places and reviews — the action stays visible, and you can undo it before it ships.

The piece that deserves a closer read is the agentic and MCP layer. A callable agent fired from a Smart Campaign, or an external assistant reaching your instance through the MCP Server, can act on its own — so the question becomes whether every action is logged, scoped, and reversible. Decide up front what an agent may touch, keep its access read-only or revocable where you can, and make sure you can trace any change back to the trigger that caused it. Our Trigger, Action, Impact breakdown and our notes on guardrail-driven automation walk through how to set those boundaries before you switch anything on.

None of this is a knock on Marketo — the predictive and assistant features are well-bounded, and the agentic pieces are new. It is simply the order of operations: define the guardrails, then grant the autonomy.

Last reviewed June 2026. Adobe Marketo Engage updates its AI capabilities frequently — confirm current specifics on the vendor's own site.
