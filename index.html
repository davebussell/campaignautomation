Where the numbers come from

A media manager moves these numbers by hand. We turn that work into a system.

A good paid-media manager already knows how to move the numbers. They mine negative keywords, match budget to what actually converts, point spend at the regions that have inventory, and rewrite ads so the keyword, the landing page, and the copy finally agree. Done by hand, each pass helps once.

We build those same workflows as automated systems. One engagement sets them up across your account, or across every account you manage if you run an agency. After that the work runs on its own, on every campaign, with no extra hours or headcount. That is why the impact keeps compounding instead of resetting each month.

Saves hours · Lifts ROAS

Negative-keyword strategy

Catch the search terms quietly draining budget and shut them off every week, not once a quarter. Less waste, more spend on the terms that actually convert.

Saves hours · Lifts ROAS

Budget aligned to performance

Move money toward the keywords and campaigns tied to real business results, and away from the ones that only look busy. Spend follows revenue, not clicks.

Saves hours · Lifts ROAS

Regional inventory matching

Point budget at the regions that can actually fulfil, and pull it from the ones that cannot. You stop paying for clicks you could never serve.

Saves hours · Lifts Quality Score

Keyword, landing page, and ad language matched

Line up the words in the keyword, the ad, and the page it lands on. Google rewards the match with a higher Quality Score, which lowers cost and widens reach.

Build vs buy

The platforms are wrappers.
The moat is fit.

Under the hood, every ad-automation platform is four things: data connectors, decision logic, execution calls through the same APIs anyone can use, and a UI. All four can now be built directly in tools like Claude and Cursor — shaped to your business instead of the vendor's roadmap.

For twenty years the advice was "use it out of the box, customize as little as possible." AI flipped that. Adaptation is now cheap to build and easy to maintain — and the unique fit is the advantage.
