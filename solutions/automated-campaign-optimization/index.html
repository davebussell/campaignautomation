The category

What it replaces

Campaign Automation AI is an autonomous optimization company — a firm whose product is optimization that runs itself, inside guardrails. The category exists because the three older ways of optimizing campaigns all fail the same way: they stop between passes.

Old mode 01

Periodic human passes

A specialist opens the account on Tuesday, makes changes, and comes back next week. Everything in between goes unwatched — and the waste accrues in the gaps.

Old mode 02

Rented platform wrappers

Another subscription promising automation inside a black box — logic you can't inspect, guardrails you don't set, and nothing you keep when the contract ends.

Old mode 03

Strategy decks

Recommendations that describe what should change but change nothing. The deck starts ageing the day it's delivered; the account keeps drifting.

The loop

How automated campaign optimization works

Every loop runs the same cycle: read live campaign data, decide inside its guardrails, act, log the change, repeat. Each logged change is written as Trigger → Action → Impact — what fired, what the system did, and what it was worth.

# one cycle — logged in the trigger/action/impact schema
read: search terms report, last 24 hours
decide: 14 terms breach the waste threshold
act: added as negative keywords (within approved bounds)
log: trigger → action → impact written to the audit trail
repeat: next read in 24 hours

The loops themselves come from our automations library of build guides — each free to unlock, and stood up yourself or with us. PPC waste intelligence finds and cuts wasted spend. Search-term intelligence mines queries into negatives and new keywords. Budget shifts move spend toward what's converting. Lead reactivation re-engages dormant CRM contacts. Campaign launch kits take a campaign from brief to live in hours, not weeks.

Governance

Inside guardrails you own

Every loop runs under bounded autonomy: it acts on its own, but only inside limits your team sets and can change. Autonomous never means unsupervised.

Guardrail 01

Spend caps

A hard ceiling on what any loop can spend or shift, per day and per account. At the cap, the loop stops and asks.

Guardrail 02

Change ceilings

Limits on how much one cycle can alter — bids, budgets, negatives — so no single run can reshape an account.

Guardrail 03

Approval thresholds

Routine changes execute; consequential ones queue for a human. You decide where that line sits, and you can move it.

Guardrail 04

Audit trail

Every action is logged with its trigger and its measured impact. "The AI did it" is never the answer — you can see exactly what happened, and why.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is this a platform?

No. Automated campaign optimization is a service, not software you rent. Every loop is built on your existing ad platforms, CRM, analytics, and data tools, and when it's handed over you own it outright — the logic, the guardrails, and the audit trail.

Do you run our campaigns?

No. We design, build, and stand up the optimization loops, then hand them over governed — with spend caps, change ceilings, approval thresholds, and an audit trail your team controls. The loops run continuously; your team owns and supervises them.

How do we start?

Start with the free Readiness Score: 13 questions, about four minutes, no login. Then the Campaign Automation Audit — the paid first sprint that diagnoses your accounts and produces the prioritised 30-day plan the build sprints follow.

How fast can a loop be live?

Most loops go from scoped to running in a 2–4 week sprint, because they're built on the stack you already run. Nothing waits on a migration, a data warehouse project, or a new platform rollout.
